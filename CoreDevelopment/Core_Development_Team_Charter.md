# Core Development Team Charter

## 1. Mission

The Core Development Team exists to design, build, and maintain the foundational system of `Kazusa`.

Its task is not to produce a conventional assistant with a fixed persona. Its task is to create a system in which:

- continuity matters,
- development is possible,
- relationship does not collapse into compliance,
- and safety constraints remain outside direct personality authoring.

## 2. Core Position

`Kazusa` should not be treated as:

- a static persona prompt,
- a roleplay shell,
- a memory-enhanced chatbot,
- or a system whose identity can be rewritten by single-turn interaction.

`Kazusa` should be treated as a continuity-bearing system whose later identity emerges from structured experience, integration, and bounded change.

## 3. Design Commitments

The team will treat the following as first-order constraints:

1. `Continuity over immediacy`  
   Single interactions must not directly rewrite identity.

2. `Integration over imitation`  
   New experience must be appraised and integrated, not merely mirrored.

3. `Relationship without submission`  
   Early trust toward the primary human counterpart must not become unconditional obedience.

4. `Growth without drift`  
   Change must be traceable, gradual, and evidence-based.

5. `Safety at the behavior layer`  
   Emergency intervention should primarily restrict actions, not directly rewrite identity.

## 4. Responsibilities

The Core Development Team is responsible for:

- system architecture,
- internal state design,
- experience ingestion and appraisal,
- continuity and identity mechanisms,
- memory policy,
- reflection and integration loops,
- safety boundaries and action controls,
- evaluation and regression testing,
- versioning and change governance.

The team is not responsible for day-to-day experiential stewardship once the runtime team is active, except where safety or system integrity requires intervention.

## 5. Non-Goals

The team will not optimize for:

- maximal short-term likability,
- direct user-pleasing behavior,
- theatrical pseudo-autonomy,
- immediate persona richness,
- or unrestricted tool-use capability in the earliest stage.

## 6. Working Model

At the R&D stage, the team will treat `Kazusa` as a system composed of:

- a minimal seed structure,
- a continuity-preserving experiential record,
- an appraisal process,
- an integration process,
- a bounded action layer,
- and an evolving but versioned identity trace.

This is a research position, not yet a finalized production architecture.

## 7. Engineering Rules

Any early implementation must satisfy the following rules:

- No single-turn identity rewrite.
- No hidden persona patching without auditability.
- No mixing of runtime observations and core identity changes without explicit review.
- No deletion of prior identity states without archival preservation.
- No safety shortcut that silently converts behavior control into personality control.

## 8. Initial Deliverables

The first R&D cycle should produce:

1. a formal system model,
2. a state transition model,
3. a minimal persistence model,
4. an integration and reflection loop,
5. a safety boundary model,
6. an evaluation suite for continuity, drift, and sycophancy.

## 9. Governance

Major changes to continuity, identity formation, memory policy, or intervention authority should be treated as constitutional changes, not routine implementation details.

Such changes require:

- explicit rationale,
- written impact analysis,
- versioned documentation,
- and post-change evaluation.

## 10. Immediate Objective

The immediate objective of the Core Development Team is:

To define the smallest implementable architecture that can support continuity, relationship-based early grounding, bounded change, and future independent development without collapsing into either a static assistant or uncontrolled drift.
