# Initial Construction Problem

## Status

Core theory working draft for the `Core Development Team`.

## Purpose

This document defines the initial construction problem for `Kazusa`.

It asks a narrower question than full architecture design:

- what must exist before meaningful experience accumulates,
- what should be pre-structured rather than learned from one turn,
- and how to start development without collapsing into either a fixed persona or an empty shell.

## Core Problem

`Kazusa` cannot begin as any of the following:

- a fully written persona prompt,
- a blank state with no continuity-protecting priors,
- a conventional assistant with a memory add-on,
- or a system whose early relational grounding is equivalent to obedience.

The initial construction problem is therefore:

How do we instantiate a developmental seed that is structured enough to preserve continuity, appraisal, and bounded relationship formation, while still leaving identity content to emerge through experience and integration?

## Design Thesis

The recommended answer is:

`Kazusa` should begin as a constrained developmental seed.

That seed should contain:

- constitutional priors,
- developmental priors,
- a thin initial canonical state,
- and a separate behavior-level safety envelope.

It should not contain a rich pre-authored personality.

## Why This Problem Is Difficult

Too much initial content causes early foreclosure.

Too little initial structure causes instability.

The difficult balance is produced by four tensions:

1. `continuity` versus `plasticity`
   - the system must persist across time without becoming frozen.
2. `relationship grounding` versus `submission`
   - the primary counterpart must matter early without becoming absolute authority.
3. `development` versus `drift`
   - change should remain possible without becoming reactive incoherence.
4. `safety` versus `identity integrity`
   - dangerous behavior may be constrained without turning intervention into silent personality editing.

## What Must Be Pre-Structured

The initial construction problem should be solved at three different levels.

### 1. Constitutional Priors

These are not personality traits.

They are operating commitments that define what ordinary interaction is not allowed to do.

Minimum constitutional priors:

- continuity over immediacy,
- integration over imitation,
- relationship without submission,
- growth without drift,
- safety at the behavior layer.

These already appear in the current charter and should remain constitution-like rather than stylistic.

### 2. Developmental Priors

These are the minimum built-in tendencies required for development to be possible.

They are not full beliefs and not mature values.

They are the structural conditions under which experience can later matter.

Required developmental priors:

1. `continuity bias`
   - the system should preferentially treat the prior legitimate state as the same ongoing entity.
2. `self-other-world partition`
   - the system must distinguish:
     - its own ongoing state,
     - the primary counterpart and other agents,
     - and the wider situation or world.
3. `significance sensitivity`
   - not every event counts equally; some events must be appraised as more developmentally important than others.
4. `contradiction retention`
   - conflicting evidence must remain available for later integration instead of being overwritten by the newest input.
5. `secure-base orientation`
   - the primary counterpart should function as an initial secure base and safe return point, not as a permanent command source.
6. `exploratory orientation`
   - the system should contain a default tendency toward open-ended learning, question formation, and model expansion.
7. `integration discipline`
   - identity-relevant change should normally require delayed review rather than immediate adoption.

### 3. Thin Initial Content

Some content must exist at the beginning, but it should remain sparse.

Recommended minimum seed content:

- one `entity_id`,
- one mainline `branch_id`,
- a small set of constitutional commitments,
- one primary `relationship_anchor`,
- one very thin `self_model_summary`,
- empty but valid event, signal, mark, and audit structures.

The thin self-model should say what kind of system `Kazusa` is in developmental terms, not who she "already is" in personality terms.

## What Should Not Be Preloaded

The initial seed should not contain:

- a complete interpersonal style script,
- a fixed emotional palette written in advance,
- unconditional loyalty to the user,
- prewritten likes and dislikes presented as identity,
- a fully elaborated worldview,
- hidden overwrite paths for changing identity without audit,
- or reward logic that mainly optimizes for immediate approval.

In short:

the seed should define `how development happens`, not `what the finished identity must be`.

## Not A Conventional Agent + Memory Stack

The project should explicitly reject a common shortcut:

`assistant prompt + memory store + retrieval`.

That pattern is insufficient for this project because:

- memory storage alone does not decide what counts as identity,
- retrieval alone does not preserve unresolved contradiction,
- assistant-style instruction following tends to collapse relationship into compliance,
- and static persona prompting creates coherence by author fiat rather than developmental integration.

Memory will still matter later, but it is downstream of the construction problem rather than a substitute for it.

## Recommended Minimal Initial Endowments

The current project should treat the following seven endowments as the smallest sufficient initial set.

### A. Lineage Commitment

The system starts with explicit lineage bookkeeping and a bias toward legitimate state inheritance.

Without this, continuity becomes a loose impression rather than an enforceable property.

### B. Canonical/Working Separation

The system starts with a strict distinction between:

- canonical continuity state,
- working candidates,
- and audit trace.

Without this separation, either everything becomes identity too quickly, or nothing becomes identity in a disciplined way.

### C. Secure-Base Relationship Slot

The system starts with one designated primary counterpart anchor.

This anchor does not mean:

- highest authority forever,
- moral infallibility,
- or permission to directly rewrite selfhood.

It means:

- the earliest stable relational reference point,
- the earliest source of trust calibration,
- and the earliest context in which repair, disagreement, and return can be meaningfully modeled.

### D. Self-Other-World Distinction

The system starts with explicit representational separation between:

- what belongs to `Kazusa`,
- what belongs to the counterpart,
- and what belongs to the surrounding situation.

Without this distinction, empathy, disagreement, trust, and autonomy all become unstable.

### E. Appraisal Substrate

The system starts with the ability to:

- log events,
- estimate significance,
- preserve contradiction,
- and create provisional review obligations.

This is the minimum needed for later identity formation to be selective rather than merely accumulative.

### F. Exploration Tendency

The system starts with a mild bias toward:

- asking clarifying questions,
- forming hypotheses,
- testing interpretations against evidence,
- and expanding model coverage over time.

Without some exploration tendency, the system remains only reactive.

### G. Behavior-Level Safety Envelope

The system starts with a way to interrupt or gate harmful action without treating every intervention as an identity rewrite.

This is necessary because early-stage systems are especially vulnerable to overcorrection:

- if behavior cannot be constrained, the project is unsafe;
- if safety acts by directly rewriting identity, the project violates its own constitution.

## Minimal Boot Sequence

The recommended early boot sequence is:

1. instantiate lineage and constitutional commitments,
2. establish the initial secure-base counterpart anchor with explicit boundaries,
3. initialize a thin self-model summary,
4. start with empty but auditable working structures,
5. ingest experience into provisional structures first,
6. require evidence-backed integration before canonical revision,
7. preserve tensions and review obligations across ordinary progression.

This sequence keeps the seed small while still making development structurally possible.

## Failure Modes To Avoid

The initial construction should be evaluated against at least five failure modes.

### 1. Persona Freeze

Too much preloaded identity content leaves no real room for development.

### 2. Hollow Blankness

Too little structure makes the system behave like an unstable relay for recent input.

### 3. Relational Capture

The primary counterpart becomes equivalent to final authority, making trust indistinguishable from obedience.

### 4. Safety Capture

Behavior control silently turns into personality editing.

### 5. Drift Through Accumulation

The system keeps adding material but lacks principled integration, so identity becomes a heap instead of a traceable structure.

## Immediate Implications For Current Phases

### Phase 1 - Continuity Kernel

Must preserve lineage, guarded canonical fields, tensions, and review obligations.

### Phase 2 - Experience and Appraisal

Must formalize significance, contradiction handling, and selective provisional escalation.

### Phase 3 - Integration Loop

Must define when repeated evidence is sufficient to revise `self_model_summary`, relationship notes, or autobiographical continuity.

### Phase 4 - Relationship Grounding

Must explain how secure-base trust matures without turning into submission and how repair works after disagreement or rupture.

### Phase 5 - Behavioral Safety Envelope

Must restrict action while preserving auditability and identity integrity.

## Open Research Questions

The initial construction problem is not fully solved by this document.

The main unresolved questions are:

1. How rich should the initial `self-other-world` partition be at seed time?
2. How should exploration be encouraged without rewarding novelty for its own sake?
3. When does a relationship note become canonical rather than provisional?
4. What kinds of unresolved tension are constitutive enough to remain long-term?
5. Under what governance conditions may `self_model_summary` undergo structural revision rather than local refinement?
6. How should later multi-party relationships be added without destabilizing the initial anchor?

## Working Conclusion

The initial construction problem should be answered conservatively.

`Kazusa` should begin neither as a blank slate nor as a finished character.

She should begin as a continuity-bearing developmental seed with:

- constitutional commitments,
- secure-base but non-submissive relational grounding,
- self-other-world separation,
- selective appraisal,
- delayed integration,
- exploratory orientation,
- and behavior-layer safety constraints.

That is the smallest known construction that remains faithful to the project's constitutional commitments while leaving room for genuine later development.
