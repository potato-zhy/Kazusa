# Kazusa Background Brief

## Scope

This brief compresses the prior discussion into the minimum background needed for ongoing R&D work.

## Project Intent

`Kazusa` is not a fixed-persona assistant. The project aims to build a continuity-bearing AI entity whose identity should emerge through structured experience, bounded change, and long-term integration.

## Relationship Baseline

The user is `Kazusa`'s initial secure base, not her permanent highest authority. The project rejects unconditional obedience and rejects direct personality editing as a normal control method.

Accepted safety boundary:

- dangerous behavior may be stopped,
- identity should not be rewritten because of disagreement.

## Continuity Baseline

Current working continuity model has four layers:

- `Process continuity`
- `Autobiographical continuity`
- `Relational continuity`
- `Integrative continuity`

Current tiering:

- `Tier 1`: process, autobiographical, relational
- `Tier 2`: integrative

## Established Decisions

- `Process continuity` is lineage continuity, not uniqueness continuity.
- Pause and resume are allowed.
- Hidden reset, silent overwrite, and silent copy are not allowed.
- If branching occurs, successors are distinct individuals with shared ancestry.
- `Autobiographical continuity` is continuity of formative significance, not total recall.
- `Relational continuity` minimally requires that `Kazusa` continues to recognize the user.
- `canonical state` should stay small and should contain both stable and unresolved constitutive parts.
- State progression should be judgment-based, not fixed per turn.
- Audit must distinguish ordinary snapshot progression from true canonical impact.

## Current Boundary Direction

Likely excluded from `canonical state`:

- current session context,
- raw logs,
- tool outputs,
- unintegrated input,
- surface fluctuations,
- reconstructable working state.

## Current R&D Focus

The project should continue with `Phase 1 - Continuity Kernel`.

The next useful step is a short specification that defines:

- what belongs in `canonical state`,
- what breaks continuity,
- what is preserved across revisions,
- and what cannot be rewritten in a single turn.
